Aimé Césaire

A literary man

Presentation

Aimé Fernand David Césaire
born 26 June 1913 - died 17 April 2008
was a French, Martinican, Afro-Caribbean poet and author.

Through his thought and poetry, he influenced African and black American intellectuals in their fight against colonialism and acculturation.

He created the literary review L'Étudiant noir (The Black Student) with his alter ego Léopold Sédar Senghor and Léon Damas with whom he coined the concept of "négritude", promoting the African culture victim of the colonialism in the French Empire in Africa.

He had several roles, especially high school teacher and as a left-wing intellectual, he also get involved in a political career. He was elected mayor of Fort-de-France (capital of Martinique, French West Indies) in 1945, then became deputy.

« Négritude is the simple recognition of the fact of being black, and the acceptance of this fact, of our destiny as black people, of our history and our culture. »
